The Best Spots!

Pier 57 is a rooftop pier located along the Hudson River. It was opened very recently in April of 2022. The rooftop portion features multiple floors to view the Hudson River to the west, as well as Little Island, another recent public project, to the south. The view is immaculate, especially as the sun sets. This park is definitely the best during summertime, but the next two spots can cover you year-round!

Pier 57

Central Park is impossible to miss if you've ever seen a map of Manhattan. Taking up 6% of the borough, Central Park offers a respite from the bustle of the city with it's rich views and preservation of nature. The park retains it's beauty year-round with new sights each season that are all worth seeing. If you're an NYC resident who loves nature, a walk through Central Park is a must.

Central Park

The Brooklyn Bridge is another great spot in NYC. A fun daytime activity is walking across the bridge from the Manhattan side into the Brooklyn neighborhood of Dumbo. Not only is the view from the bridge fascinating, but the surrounding neighborhood in Brooklyn has wonderful piers and restaurants.
